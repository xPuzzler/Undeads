Why Stake at All?

From Idle to Yield-Bearing

You hold a BasedUndead. It just sits there. Someone buys another one for 1 ETH on OpenSea, the creator earns 0.075 ETH royalty. Without staking, that royalty goes 100% to the creator wallet.

With staking active, half of every royalty flows directly to people who locked their NFTs. The contract handles everything onchain. No admin discretion, no off-chain spreadsheets, no trust required.

The catch: while you're staked, your NFT is locked in the contract. You can't sell it, transfer it, or use it anywhere else. You're trading liquidity for yield.

The Mechanics

The Math, Plain English

When a royalty arrives at the staking contract, here's what happens:

Sale: 1.0 ETH on OpenSea └─ Royalty: 0.075 ETH (7.5%) ├─ 0.0375 ETH → creator wallet (instant) └─ 0.0375 ETH → reward pool └─ Distributed by stake share (your NFTs ÷ total staked NFTs)

The reward pool isn't actually held in a literal "pool", it uses a math trick called the Synthetix pull pattern. Instead of paying every staker individually (which would cost a fortune in gas every sale), the contract just bumps a global counter. Each user's earnings are calculated the moment they ask, based on how that counter has moved since they last interacted.

Result: gas costs stay constant whether there are 5 stakers or 5,000.

04
ALICE EXITS

Alice unstakes all 250 (24h+ later)

The lock has expired. When unstake() runs, the contract automatically:

What Happens, In Order 1. Calculates Alice's total earned based on her old weight (250)
2. Locks that amount into pendingRewards[Alice], preserved
3. Returns her 250 NFTs
4. stakedBalance[Alice] = 0, totalStaked drops to 350

Alice now has her 250 NFTs back in her wallet (transferable, sellable), plus an accumulated pendingRewards balance she can claim anytime.

She does NOT lose her unclaimed rewards by unstaking. They sit there until she calls claim().

05
ALICE RETURNS

Alice re-stakes without claiming

Alice changes her mind and re-stakes (without claiming first).

What Resets, What Doesn't stakeTimestamp resets→24h lock restarts
pendingRewards from Phase 4→untouched, still claimable
Future earned→adds on top of pending balance

Translation: re-staking doesn't reset rewards. Only the lock timer resets.

06
ALICE ADDS MORE

24h+ later, Alice adds 10 more NFTs

Alice has 250 staked. She just bought 10 more and wants to stake them.

After Adding 10 More stakedBalance[Alice] = 260
totalStaked += 10
stakeTimestamp resets→24h lock applies to ALL 260
This is the most counterintuitive rule. Alice cannot unstake any of her 260 NFTs (including the original 250 that were already past their lock) until 24 hours pass from this new stake action.

The stakeTimestamp field is per-wallet, not per-NFT. Adding more stake = resetting the entire timer.
07
THE HORDE GROWS

More holders join the pool

Dave, Eve, Frank, they all stake. The pool grows. Each new staker:

For Every New Staker • Gets a share = their NFTs / new total
• Only earns from royalties arriving after they stake
• Locks themselves for 24 hours

Existing stakers' earned balances are unaffected by new joiners, but their share percentage drops as the pool grows. If you held 5% when the pool was 1000 NFTs, you'd hold 2.5% when the pool reaches 2000 NFTs (assuming you don't add more stake).

Nothing breaks. The math always sums to 100%.

Common Questions

Things People Always Ask

Click any question to expand the answer. Covers every scenario you'll likely hit.

When do I start earning rewards after I stake?
Immediately. The reward math only looks at how many NFTs you have staked — not how long they've been there. The moment your stake transaction confirms, you're earning your share of any royalties that arrive afterward.

The 24h lock is purely about when you can unstake — it has zero effect on earnings. You could stake at 3:00 PM, see a sale happen at 3:01 PM, and watch your earned balance tick up immediately. You can even claim() those rewards before the lock expires — claiming and unstaking are two separate things.
If I claim rewards, does my staking position change?
No. Claim only takes the ETH out, your NFTs stay staked, your lock stays where it is.
If I unstake, do I lose my unclaimed rewards?
No. The contract automatically preserves them. You can claim them anytime, even if you have zero NFTs staked.
Can I stake at any time, or only at launch?
Anytime. The pool is permanent and ongoing.
Why does the dashboard ask me to sign an "approval" the first time I stake?
NFTs use a permission system. Before the staking contract can move your NFTs out of your wallet, you have to grant it permission once via setApprovalForAll. After that single signature, every future stake works without re-approval.

This is standard for every NFT marketplace and staking platform, same pattern OpenSea, Magic Eden, and others use. The approval is for the staking contract specifically, not a blanket permission for anyone else. You can revoke it any time by calling setApprovalForAll(stakingContract, false) if you want — though you'd just have to re-approve to stake again.
What if no one is staking when a royalty arrives?
The full royalty (100%) goes to the creator wallet. Stakers can't earn from a pool with no stakers, so the contract sensibly routes everything to the creator. No ETH is ever wasted or trapped.
Why does adding more NFTs reset my full lock?
The contract tracks one stakeTimestamp per wallet, not per NFT. This keeps the contract simple and gas-efficient. The tradeoff: think carefully before adding to a stake, you're committing the whole position for another 24 hours.
Can I stake a subset and keep some in my wallet?
Yes. You only stake the token IDs you choose. The rest stay liquid.
Can I sell a staked NFT?
No. The contract owns it while it's staked. You'd have to unstake first (and pass the 24h lock), then sell.
Can I claim my rewards and stake more NFTs in the same transaction?
Yes. There's a stakeAndClaim() function that pays out any pending rewards and stakes new NFTs in a single transaction. Saves gas vs. doing both separately.

Heads up: the 24h lock will still reset, because adding more stake always counts as a fresh stake action. But at least you only pay gas once. The dashboard will offer this option automatically when you have both unclaimed rewards and NFTs selected to stake.
What's the gas cost?
Stake: ~80k gas per NFT (or ~50k each in a batch).
Unstake: similar.
Claim: ~50k gas regardless of accumulated amount.

On Base, that's typically pennies in ETH.
Where do my rewards go when I claim?
Direct ETH transfer to your wallet. Same wallet you're staking from.
Can the team take my staked NFTs?
No. The staking contract is non-upgradeable and only YOU can call unstake() for your tokens. The contract owner can update the royalty receiver address (used to redirect royalties to a redeployed staking contract if ever needed) but cannot touch your NFTs or your earned rewards.
What if I lose my wallet or private key?
Your staked NFTs and unclaimed rewards are tied to that wallet address forever. Only YOU (or anyone with your private key) can call unstake() or claim() for that wallet.

The team has no admin override to recover NFTs from a lost wallet. This is a feature, not a bug it's why the staking contract is genuinely trustless. But it means you should treat your wallet security as protecting both your NFTs and your accumulated rewards.

Use a hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) for any wallet holding significant NFTs or staking positions. Back up your seed phrase. Never share it with anyone — not even people claiming to be from the team.
What if the staking contract gets redeployed?
You'd be given a migration window. During that window, you must:
1. Call claim() to take your rewards.
2. Call unstake() (after 24h lock passes) to get your NFTs back.
3. Stake fresh into the new contract.

There is no automated migration, only YOU control your staked NFTs. The team will announce migrations well in advance.

Pro Tip

A Word on Lock Timing

If you want to stake NFTs in waves and avoid restarting your lock unnecessarily, plan ahead.

Stake in batches, not in dribbles

✗ Don't Stake 100 today, stake 50 tomorrow. The entire 150 will be locked for 24h after tomorrow's stake, you've effectively delayed your unlock by a day.
✓ Do Stake all 150 at once. Single 24h lock. Clean exit.

If your stake plan is "buy more over the next month," consider waiting until you're done buying before staking, or accept that each new stake adds another 24h to the clock for the whole position. You can also treat future Undeads as a separate "round" and keep them in your wallet until you have a clean batch ready.

Final Mental Model

A Yield-Bearing Locker

The Locker Analogy

Think of staking like a yield-bearing locker.

You put NFTs in.

The lock door says: "24h after the most recent deposit."

While locked, the locker pays you ETH based on royalties from secondary sales.

You can withdraw the ETH anytime through a window in the door (claim).

You can take the NFTs out only after the lock door opens.

Adding new NFTs bolts the door shut for another 24h.

Taking some NFTs out doesn't reset the lock, once open, it stays open until you add more.

That's it. Welcome to the horde.
