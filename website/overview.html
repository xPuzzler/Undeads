The Collection

6,666 Souls

A horde of skeletal warriors, phantom wanderers, undead rulers, and forgotten kings — each one rendered pixel by pixel as a one-of-a-kind sprite. Some are common foot-soldiers of the army. Others are 1-of-1 legends with traits no other Undead carries.

Every Undead has up to 8 traits: Background, Type, Clothes, Eyes, Eyewear, Head, Mask, and Mouth. The combinations produce a wide spectrum — the foot-soldier in dirty rags is as much a part of the horde as the gilded vampire king. Trait rarity is preserved permanently in the renderer contract, so the on-chain record itself is the source of truth for which traits are common and which are rare.

The art is a deliberate departure for the artist — SAM's first venture into pixel art, learned sprite by sprite, shadow by shadow. The result is a collection with a unified gothic aesthetic: blood-red palettes, candlelit highlights, decay rendered in gold and bone. Look at one Undead and you'll know what tribe it belongs to.

The Architecture

Fully Onchain. No Exceptions.

Most NFT collections store art on IPFS or centralized servers, leaving the "permanent" part hanging on a thread. Based Undeads stores everything on Base directly — split across four contracts that work together to render a complete NFT entirely from chain state.

Layer 1 of 4

Storage Contract

Holds the raw PNG bytes for every Undead, deployed as compressed SSTORE2 blobs. Each token's image is fetched directly from chain — no external lookup, no proxy, no decay.

Layer 2 of 4

Renderer Contract

Turns raw bytes into full ERC-721 metadata at request time. Calls tokenURI() and you get a base64 data URI — image, name, description, traits — assembled live, never cached off-chain.

Layer 3 of 4

NFT Contract

Standard ERC-721 with ERC-2981 royalties baked in. Routes 7.5% of every secondary sale to the staking contract. Frozen post-mint: art is locked, supply is locked, the collection is finalized.

Layer 4 of 4

Staking Contract

The yield engine. Receives every royalty, splits 50/50 between creator and stakers, distributes to lockers using the Synthetix pull pattern — gas-efficient at any scale, mathematically airtight.

The Old Way

  • Art hosted on IPFS — pinned by a service that may stop tomorrow
  • Metadata served from a website — taken offline = NFT goes blank
  • Centralized image gateways throttle, cache, censor
  • "Forever" depends on someone paying a hosting bill
  • Disappears the moment the team walks away

The Undead Way

  • PNG bytes stored as immutable contract code on Base
  • Metadata generated fresh from chain on every tokenURI call
  • No external dependencies of any kind — IPFS, servers, all gone
  • "Forever" really means as long as Base exists
  • Survives the team, the website, the founders entirely

The Stack

Four Contracts. One Horde.

Every piece of the protocol is open and verifiable on Basescan. Hover the cards below to see what each contract does. Storage and NFT contracts are frozen — the renderer is intentionally upgradable so we can ship better art tooling later, while the underlying art and supply stay locked.

UndeadStorage
Stores raw PNG bytes for every Undead.
SSTORE2 blob contracts hold compressed pixel data on-chain. Frozen post-upload — art cannot be modified.
UndeadRenderer
Builds full metadata at tokenURI() time.
Reads from Storage, assembles base64 data URI with image + traits. Owner-controlled to allow future upgrades.
UndeadNFT
ERC-721 + ERC-2981 royalties, points to renderer.
Standard token contract with built-in 7.5% royalty routing to the staking contract. Frozen — supply, art, and royalty target are locked.
UndeadStaking
Receives royalties, distributes to stakers and creator.
50/50 split. Synthetix pull pattern keeps gas constant at any pool size. Replaceable if ever needed via royalty receiver swap.
