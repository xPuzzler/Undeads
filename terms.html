What Based Undeads Is

Based Undeads is a fully decentralized, on-chain raffle protocol deployed on the Base blockchain. It allows any wallet holder to create a raffle for any ERC721 NFT they own, and allows other wallet holders to purchase entry tickets priced at $1 USD worth of ETH each.

Based Undeads is not a lottery operator, financial institution, or regulated gambling service. It is a smart contract protocol. The smart contract code is immutable once deployed and executes automatically without human intervention.

The platform interface (this website) is a frontend only. It does not hold custody of your funds or NFTs at any time. All assets are held exclusively by the on-chain smart contract. The platform team does not control game outcomes, ticket sales, or fund distribution.

How Games Work — Full Rules

The following rules are enforced entirely by the smart contract and cannot be overridden by the platform team or game creators:

  • Game creation: A creator deposits an ERC721 NFT into the smart contract escrow. The NFT is locked until the game concludes.
  • Ticket pricing: Every ticket costs exactly $1.00 USD worth of ETH, calculated live from the Chainlink ETH/USD price oracle on Base mainnet.
  • Duration: Games run for a creator-defined period between 1 hour and 7 days. The end time is set at creation and cannot be changed.
  • Ticket limit: A maximum of 100 tickets may be purchased per transaction. There is no per-wallet cap on total tickets.
  • Winner selection: One winner is selected algorithmically from all ticket holders after the game ends. The selection uses on-chain entropy. It is transparent, verifiable, and cannot be manually influenced by the platform.
  • NFT delivery: Upon winner selection, the smart contract transfers the escrowed NFT directly to the winner's wallet. No manual claim is required.
  • Creator payout: The creator receives 90% of all ticket sale revenue instantly as each ticket is sold. The platform receives 10%.
  • No cancellation after ticket sales: Once any ticket is sold, the game cannot be cancelled. The creator has already received their payout. The game MUST resolve via the draw.
  • Zero-ticket reclaim: If a game ends with zero tickets sold, only the creator may reclaim their NFT. No ETH was collected.

Important: Anyone can trigger the winner draw after a game ends — not just the creator. If the creator does not call drawWinner, any other participant or third party can do so. The contract does not require creator involvement to conclude a game.

Ticket Purchases & Pricing

Ticket prices are denominated in ETH and calculated at transaction time from the Chainlink price oracle. Due to ETH price volatility, the exact ETH amount may differ from previous estimates. The contract accepts payments within 1% of the precise price to account for price movement between quote and settlement.

  • All purchases are final. There are no refunds under any circumstances once a transaction is confirmed on-chain.
  • Overpayment: Any ETH sent above the ticket cost is automatically refunded in the same transaction.
  • Underpayment: Transactions that underpay by more than 1% are automatically reverted. No tickets are issued and no funds are deducted.
  • Network fees: Blockchain gas fees are separate from ticket prices. Gas is paid to the Base network, not to Based Undeads. Gas fees are non-refundable even if a transaction reverts.
  • You bear all price risk. If the ETH price drops significantly, your tickets purchased at a higher price still cost $1 USD equivalent. No adjustment is made post-purchase.

Prize Claims & NFT Delivery

The smart contract transfers the prize NFT directly to the winner's wallet address during the drawWinner transaction. No claim action is required from the winner.

  • Winners are determined by the on-chain selection algorithm. Results are final and cannot be disputed.
  • The platform cannot manually override, change, or reassign a winner.
  • If the winner's wallet cannot receive the NFT (e.g. a broken contract wallet), the draw transaction may fail. In this case, the emergency recovery function may be invoked by the platform owner after a 30-day delay — the NFT is then held by the platform owner who will make reasonable efforts to resolve delivery.
  • Based Undeads makes no warranty that an NFT prize has the value stated by the game creator. Asset values are self-reported and unverified.

Randomness & Fairness

Winner selection uses a combination of on-chain entropy sources including Ethereum RANDAO (block.prevrandao) and block hash data. This method is transparent and publicly verifiable on-chain.

This is not cryptographically perfect randomness. On Base mainnet, the sequencer has theoretical ability to influence block timing. For the low prize values typical of a $1 game, this attack is economically irrational. However:

Based Undeads does not guarantee that winner selection is perfectly unbiasable. By purchasing tickets you accept the randomness methodology as described. For high-value games, you should consider whether the prize value justifies the theoretical manipulation risk.

All ticket ranges, game parameters, and winner indices are publicly accessible on-chain. Any participant can independently verify the draw result.
